3) Project Description
   Our application, Taylor's Student Wellness and Nutrition App, is a mobile application centered on helping the Taylor's student community by allowing them to record and      track their daily meals as well as being more aware of its nutritional content through our applications available features such as the tracking of meal calories, water      intake and budget use, searchable campus meal database, quick add meal feature and many more, all in line with our chosen social development goal, that being SDG 3: Good    health and well-being.
   
5) Overview of SDLC
   
   a) Requirements gathering
      - We created and distributed a Google Form in order to gather requirements from our staekholders (mainly students). From this survey, we identified the most                   chosen/voted answers and gathered up the results in preparation for analysis.
   
   b) Analysis
      - Based on the gathered requirements, we analysed them and identified the functional and non-functional requirements of our system, consisting of features such as the         quick add meal system or searchable campus meal database. After this analysis, we created user stories based on these requirements and prioritized them in our               product backlog using MoSCoW method in preparation for our first sprint.
   
   c) Design
      - Our first sprint began with the design phase in which we created the use case diagram which represents how our end users will interact with our application as well          as the class diagram which serves as the blueprint for our applciation, with both being created using Visual Paradigm.
   
   d) Implementation
      - In the first sprint (right after design), we prioritized Must Have features first and created the wireframes and early prototype of our application using Figma,             allowing our end users to visualise what our application will be. During the second sprint (right after testing/feedback), we incorporated the users feedback from           testing as well as prioritized Should Have and Could Have features to create our final prototype.
   
   e) Testing/Feedback
      - After completing the early prototype, we conducted testing by creating an expansive list of possible test cases to ensure every possible feature would function              properly and work well. To obtain user feedback for the second sprint, we created a Google Form to allow end users to interact with and provide feedback on our              early prototype. We also conducted another round of testing on the final prototype before deployment.
